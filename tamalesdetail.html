Ingredients

4 cups Masa harina corn flour
3 cups Broth (beef, chicken or vegetable broth)
2 tablespoons Baking powder
1 teaspoon Salt
1 teaspoon Cumin
1 1/3 cups Lard
Red chili pork
2 cups Sales verde chicken
1.5 cups Bean and cheese
1 cup Authentic Mexican rice, for serving

Steps

  1. Soak the corn husks in a bowl of very hot water for 30 minutes or until softened
  2. Prepare desired fillings
  3. Make the tamal dough: In a large bowl, use an electric mixer to beat the lard and 2 tablespoons of broth until fluffy, about 3-5 minutes
  4. Combine the masa flour, baking powder, salt, and cumin in a separate bowl; stir into the lard mixture and beat well with an electric mixer
  5. Add the broth, little by little to form a very soft dough. Beat on high speed for several minutes
  6. Assemble the tamales: Lay a corn husk, glossy side up, on the counter with the wide end at the top
  7. Scoop about ¬º cup of dough onto the top, center of the corn husk.
  8. Lay a piece of plastic wrap over the dough and use your hands to press and spread the masa into a thin layer, about ¬º inch thick
  9. Keep the dough spread along the top half of the corn husk to allow plenty of room to fold the bottom husk up, when its time
  10. Place 1-2 tablespoons of desired filling in a line down the center of the dough
  11. Fold in one long side of the husk over the filling. Fold in the other long side, overlapping the first (like folding a brochure). Fold the bottom of the husk up
  12. Tying tamales (optional): You don‚Äôt have to tie a corn husk string around the tamales--it does take more time and they will hold together without it
  13. Cook on the stove-top or Instant Pot: Add water to the bottom of your steamer or instant pot. (About 1 cup for IP and a few cups for a steamer pot‚Äîdon‚Äôt fill above the steamer rack.)
  14. Place tamales standing upright, with their open end up, just tightly enough to keep them standing
  15. Steamer: Bring water to a boil (in Mexico they would often place a coin at the bottom of thesteamer and when the coin started to tap in the pot you know the water was boiling.)
  16. Once boiling, reduce to a simmer and steam for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or longer. Check them after 45 minutes
  17. To test if the tamales are done: Remove one and try to pull the husk off. If the husk pulls away cleanly from the tamale they're done
  18. If the dough is still sticky or wet looking, cook them for 5-10 minutes longer and try again
  19. Store leftover tamales in the refrigerator for 5-7 days depending on the freshness of your ingredients
